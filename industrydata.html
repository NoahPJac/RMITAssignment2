Our Job Titles & Our Ranks

Our group is composed of 4 members that have 4 different ideal jobs. Burning Glass offered us two different graphs about IT titles called respectively “Top Occupations 2018” and “Top IT Job Titles 2018” and two graphs about skills required named “Top IT skills” and Top Generic Skills”.

Alex’s and Noah’s ideal jobs are located in both graphs about IT titles, while Jack’s and Jayden’s are in one.

The title of Alex’s ideal job is “Software Engineer” which is ranked in first place through the Top Occupations and eleventh through the Top IT Job Titles.

Noah’s ideal job title is “Junior Web Developer” that is located sixth in the Top Occupations chart and fifteenth in the Top IT Job Titles.

Jack wants to become a “Lead PHP Developer”. This title is situated in twenty-fourth in the Top IT Job Titles.

Jayden’ ideal job title is Cyber Security Technician, that is in thirteenth through the Top Occupations.


Skill Sets Required For Our Jobs

Every job has various skills that are required to be able to work in that position. In the IT field we can divide the skills in General and IT skills. As you can probably spot in the table, some of these are important for more than one IT title. The most common generic skills in our four ideal jobs are Communication and Problem Solving. Communication is ranked first in the Burning Glass top generic skills graph, shared by Alex, Jack and Jayden, while Problem Solving is ranked second and shared by Alex, Jayden and Noah.


IT-Specific Skills We Require

The most common IT skills are JavaScript (Ranked second) and Website Production (Ranked seventeenth) shared both by Jack and Noah, Java (Ranked third) and Software engineering (Ranked fifteenth) shared by Jayden and Alex, Linux (Ranked thirteenth) used by Noah and Jayden and SQL (Ranked first) used by Jack and Jayden

Then there are other skills such as Python (Ranked twenty-second) and Microsoft C# (Ranked twelfth) included in Alex’s skills set and ITIL (Ranked twentieth) included in Jayden’s required skills set.

Just as there are required skills, there are also some skills that are not necessary. As shown in the table, there is one IT skill and one generic skill that none of us is required to have, that is Microsoft Windows (Ranked fourth) and Writing (also Ranked fourth).


General Skills We Require

The other skills that are included in Alex’s required skills set are Organization (Ranked third), Leadership (Ranked eleventh), Team Work/Collaboration (Ranked fifth), Team Building (Ranked eighteenth) and Troubleshooting (Ranked sixth).

Jack’s ideal Job also requires Leadership (Ranked eleventh), Time Management (Ranked twelfth) Organization Skills (Ranked third) and Planning (Ranked seventh).

Noah’s skill set includes Troubleshooting (Ranked sixth), Creativity (Ranked ninth) and Multi-Tasking (Ranked twentieth).

Other skills required by Jayden’s ideal job are Team Work/Collaboration (Ranked fourth) and Time Management (Ranked twelfth).


High Ranking IT-Skills We Dont Require

Alex does not require IT skills such as SQL (Ranked first) and JavaScript (Ranked second) and generic skills such as Planning (Ranked seventh) and Detail-Oriented thinking (Ranked eighth)

Jack instead is not required to have skills with Java (Ranked third) and Project Management (Ranked fifth) and generic skills such as Problem Solving (Ranked second) and Team Work/Collaboration (Ranked fifth)

The other skills that Jayden is not required to have are JavaScript (Ranked second) and Project Management (Ranked fifth) for the IT section and Organization Skills (Ranked third) and Troubleshooting (Ranked sixth) for the general skills.

The IT skills that Noah is not required to have are SQL (Ranked first) and Java (Ranked third), while the top generic skills not required are Communication Skills (Ranked first) and Organization Skills (Ranked third).
